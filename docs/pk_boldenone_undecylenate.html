Absorption

After intramuscular injection, boldenone undecylenate is slowly released and hydrolysed to active boldenone. It is highly lipophilic, extensively protein-bound, and primarily metabolised in the liver. Metabolites are excreted mainly in urine.

Distribution

Boldenone undecylenate is completely bioavailable after administration. It undergoes slow release from the depot, dissociating into Boldenone and undecylenic acid.

Metabolism

In patients with renal impairment, the biotransformation of Boldenone differs qualitatively from that in healthy individuals. The absolute bioavailability of boldenone undecylenate is 100%. The active ingredient, boldenone, presumably becomes completely free from the steroid ester.

Elimination

Unchanged boldenone undecylenate is not eliminated via the kidneys. The steroid ester is completely hydrolyzed, and the resulting Boldenone is rapidly metabolized. Boldenone undecylenate has a terminal elimination half-life of approximately 14 days.

Excretion

Its metabolites are excreted via the kidneys (80%) and bile (20%). Boldenone does not convert into estrogenic metabolites.
