Absorption

Stanozolol is readily absorbed after oral administration and is also available as an intramuscular aqueous suspension. Oral bioavailability is relatively high for an anabolic steroid due to its 17α-alkylated structure, which allows it to resist extensive first-pass hepatic metabolism. Peak plasma concentrations are typically reached within several hours following oral dosing.

Distribution

Following absorption, stanozolol is widely distributed throughout body tissues. Unlike many anabolic steroids, it exhibits relatively low affinity for sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG), resulting in a higher proportion of circulating free drug. Plasma protein binding is moderate, and the apparent volume of distribution is consistent with extensive tissue uptake.

Metabolism

Stanozolol undergoes extensive hepatic metabolism through multiple enzymatic pathways, producing numerous hydroxylated and conjugated metabolites. The parent compound and metabolites can be detected in biological samples for extended periods due to the formation of long-lasting urinary metabolites.

Elimination

The elimination half-life of oral stanozolol is approximately 8–9 hours, although reported values vary between studies. The intramuscular suspension may exhibit a longer apparent duration of action due to slower absorption from the injection site. Parent drug concentrations decline relatively rapidly after administration.

Excretion

Stanozolol metabolites are excreted primarily via the kidneys, with a smaller proportion eliminated through the biliary route. Excretion occurs mainly as conjugated metabolites. Stanozolol does not undergo aromatization and therefore does not produce estrogenic metabolites.
