Absorption

Methenolone enanthate is slowly released from the intramuscular oil depot. After deep intramuscular injection, the ester is absorbed with a half-life of approximately 4 days.

Distribution

Metenolone enanthate is completely bioavailable after administration. It undergoes slow release from the depot, dissociating into metenolone and enanthate acid. Maximum plasma concentrations of metenolone and its metabolites, approximately 250 ng/ml, are reached 6–11 days after injection, with plasma levels subsequently decreasing slowly.

Metabolism

In patients with renal impairment, the biotransformation of metenolone differs qualitatively from that in healthy individuals. The absolute bioavailability of metenolone enanthate is 100%. The active ingredient, metenolone, presumably becomes completely free from the steroid ester.

Elimination

Unchanged metenolone enanthate is not eliminated via the kidneys. The steroid ester is completely hydrolyzed, and the resulting metenolone is rapidly metabolized.

Excretion

Its metabolites are excreted via the kidneys (80%) and bile (20%). Metenolone does not convert into estrogenic metabolites.
