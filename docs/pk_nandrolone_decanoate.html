Absorption

Nandrolone decanoate is slowly released from the intramuscular oil depot. After deep intramuscular injection, the ester is absorbed with a half-life of approximately 6 days.

Pharmacokinetic parameters of nandrolone following a single-dose intramuscular administration of nandrolone decanoate 50 mg, 100 mg, and 150 mg in healthy adult males are summarized in the table below (data adapted from clinical studies):

Dose Tmax (hr) Cmax (ng/dL) Terminal Half-life (days)
50 mg 30 (median) 214 (± ~50) ~7.1
100 mg 30 (median) 426 (± ~80) ~11.7
150 mg 72 (median) 516 (± ~100) ~11.8

Note: Nandrolone decanoate is rapidly hydrolysed by esterases in blood to active nandrolone (hydrolysis half-life ≤ 1 hour). The values above reflect serum nandrolone levels after hydrolysis.

Distribution

Nandrolone is highly lipophilic and extensively bound to plasma proteins, primarily albumin and sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG).

Metabolism

Nandrolone is metabolised in the liver primarily via reduction. The main metabolites are 19-norandrosterone and 19-noretiocholanolone, which are excreted as glucuronide and sulfate conjugates.

Elimination

The terminal elimination half-life of nandrolone after nandrolone decanoate injection is approximately 6–12 days. The combined half-life of hydrolysis, distribution, and elimination is ~4.3 hours.

Excretion

Nandrolone metabolites are primarily excreted in the urine. Detectable urinary metabolites (19-norandrosterone and 19-noretiocholanolone) can persist for 3–6 weeks (or up to 6 months in some individuals) after a single injection.
