Therapeutic Indications

is used to support healing processes or prevent the harmful effects of catabolic processes. For example:

  • during convalescence
  • postoperative treatment
  • degenerative diseases
  • cachexia
  • radiation and cytostatic therapy
  • advanced breast and genital cancers in women
  • hematopoietic disorders
  • long-term corticosteroid treatment,
  • osteoporosis
  • protein deficiency in old age
  • chronic liver diseases

Not indicated for enhancement of muscle mass or athletic performance in healthy individuals.
Dosage and Administration
  • Adult males: 100mg of intramuscularly every 2 weeks.
  • In very thin women with a weight lower than their status, the dose of 50mg of intramuscular should not be exceeded every 2-3 weeks.
  • Teens with body weight greater than 50 kg (15-18 years): 50mg every 2 weeks.
Contraindications
  • Hypersensitivity to or excipients
  • Male patients with carcinoma of the breast or with known or suspected carcinoma of the prostate.
  • Prostate or male breast carcinoma
  • Hypercalcaemia or past/present liver tumours
  • Pregnancy, because of masculinization of the fetus.
  • Nephrosis or the nephrotic phase of nephritis.
  • Children under 3 years (contains benzyl alcohol)

Patients with renal failure, hepatic failure, heart failure, coronary insufficiency, a history of myocardial infarction, arterial hypertension, edema, epilepsy, migraine, benign prostatic hypertrophy, or urethral obstruction should be monitored with special precautions.

Liver function tests should be monitored regularly. A clinically significant increase in liver function parameters or cholestasis necessitates discontinuation of .

Special Warnings and Precautions
  • Exclude prostate cancer before therapy. Regular monitoring required.
  • Monitor Hb/Hct, PSA, LFTs, and lipids.
  • Inject very slowly to avoid pulmonary microembolism.
  • Caution in cardiovascular disease, sleep apnoea, and hepatic/renal impairment.

BLOOD LIPID CHANGES THAT ARE KNOWN TO BE ASSOCIATED WITH INCREASED RISK OF ATHEROSCLEROSIS ARE SEEN IN PATIENTS TREATED WITH ANDROGENS AND ANABOLIC STEROIDS. THESE CHANGES INCLUDE DECREASED HIGH-DENSITY LIPOPROTEIN AND SOMETIMES INCREASED LOW-DENSITY LIPOPROTEIN. THE CHANGES MAY BE VERY MARKED AND COULD HAVE A SERIOUS IMPACT ON THE RISK OF ATHEROSCLEROSIS AND CORONARY ARTERY DISEASE.
Fertility, Pregnancy and Lactation

Suppresses spermatogenesis (usually reversible). Contraindicated in pregnancy and breastfeeding.
Adverse Effects
Reproductive / Endocrine
  • Suppression of endogenous testosterone production
  • Testicular atrophy
  • Reduced sperm count (oligospermia)
  • Reduced fertility
  • Changes in libido (increased or decreased)
Cardiovascular & Lipids
  • Decreased HDL cholesterol
  • Increased LDL cholesterol
  • Potential increase in cardiovascular risk with prolonged use
  • Possible elevation in blood pressure
Skin & Hair
  • Acne
  • Oily skin
  • Acceleration of male-pattern hair loss in genetically susceptible individuals
Central Nervous System
  • Mood changes
  • Irritability
  • Insomnia
Women (Virilization)
  • Deepening of the voice
  • Clitoral enlargement
  • Increased body or facial hair growth
  • Menstrual irregularities
  • Some changes may be irreversible
Musculoskeletal & Laboratory Findings
  • Increased muscle mass and strength
  • Elevated creatine kinase (CK/CPK)
  • Increased serum creatinine due to greater muscle mass
Pediatric Use
  • Premature closure of growth plates
  • Impaired final adult height
Hepatic
  • Liver toxicity is considered uncommon with injectable methenolone enanthate
  • Rare liver-related adverse effects cannot be excluded
Overdose

Supportive treatment. Low acute toxicity.
Storage and Handling
  • Store below 25°C. Protect from light.
  • Shelf life: Up to 5 years.
  • Use immediately after opening.
