Benzyl Alcohol is a co-solvent and preservative. Commonly used in bacteriostatic water at 0.9% v.v.

In parenatal formulations it's used up to 10% [1] v.v. A more common range is 1.5% -2.5%.

It can be used as a co-solvent in combination with Benzyl Benzoate to formulate solutions of hormones with poor absorption. Such as testosterone base or other non-esterified hormones.

In the UGL scene it is commonly formulated up to 6% with Benzyl Benzoate up to 40%. The solvent power of Benzyl Alcohol is much stronger than Benzyl Benzoate[4].

The vapour point of Benzyl Alcohol is quite low, so when formulating it's important that it is added when the solution is cooling.

Unlike Benzyl Benzoate which extends the life of the depot, Benzyl Alcohol has been shown to increase the absorption rate and dispersion of the hormone [2][3].
